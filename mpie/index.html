About

Welcome to the official website of the MPIE Conference, an exciting interdisciplinary gathering at the University of Sussex. This event aims to unite researchers from the fields of mathematics, physics, informatics, and engineering, fostering collaboration and innovation at the intersection of these dynamic disciplines. Join us as we explore cutting-edge developments and share insights that push the boundaries of knowledge and technology. The poster exhibition is open to keen undergraduate finalists, masters, PhD and above. This is a great way for research groups to show off what they are doing, or individuals. We encourage bringing demos along if possible. The conference is broken up into four sessions: Data science, Computing approaches, Materials, and Modelling life.

Funding bodies: Be.AI Leverhulme Trust, Sussex Centre for Quantum Technologies, School of Engineering and Informatics

MPIE conference, 6th September 2024, 09:00 - 17:30

Call for posters

We are excited to announce our call for posters for the upcoming MPIE conference held here at Sussex.

The poster session will provide an excellent opportunity for students and researchers to present early-stage, pre-submitted, not-yet-ready-for-prime-time research and receive valuable feedback on their ongoing research from the community. In addition, we strongly encourage research groups to make posters to promote what their group does to the rest of the scientific community here at Sussex.

As a guideline, the poster should carry the same information as a two-page extended abstract. To that effect, provide an introduction (outline) and a summary or conclusion for your poster. Also, prepare your poster carefully so that it can be used as the basis to explain and answer questions from the viewers.



Poster deadline: 1st August 2024EXTENDED: 22cnd August

Poster format:
  1. The maximum dimension of the poster panel is A1.
  2. File format: PDF (Please limit the size of the PDF file to no more than 4 MB).
  3. There is no specific template for the posters. You should choose the format that best represents your research work. However, the posters must be printable in A1
